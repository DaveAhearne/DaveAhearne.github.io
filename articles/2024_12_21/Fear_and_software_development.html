The fear of percieved failure, software development & the tutorial trap.

Teaching children to draw seems to be much easier than teaching adults to draw.

If you give a child a set of crayons or paintbrushes they will usually have already enthusiastically picked them up and started to decorate the canvas (or anything else nearby) before you can even begin to explain that they have the freedom to draw literally anything that they like.

But adults? Adults are different.

There is even a name for the anxiety that comes from this. "Blank Page Syndrome". But why don't children have this?

The Fear of Failure

The reason that children have this approach is that they have not yet been burned by failure. The hand that touches the hot stove will not touch it again, but the hand that has never touched the stove is usually pretty enthusiastic about the whole thing...

But adults know it well, they know how much it hurts because of experiences like:

  • Working really hard on something, only for it to be mocked. "What is this, did you even try?"
  • Seeing someone else be an expert at a particular skill or hobby, and feeling devastated as a beginner because you can't do the most "simple" things
  • c

The problem is that while the strategies that help us avoid physical pain (and a burned hand) inhibit our ability to learn and grow. learning is painful and uncomfortable. It is the process of taking what was familiar, and struggling with a new concept, discarding what was familiar and contending with the unknown.

Retreat to Process and Familiarity

What is List Comprehension?

List comprehension is a compact way to process and create lists in Python. It’s an elegant alternative to using loops for creating lists, and it can significantly reduce the number of lines in your code. 

squares = [x ** 2 for x in range(10)]
print(squares)

The output will be: 

[0, 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81]

In this fake placeholder content example, the list comprehension [x ** 2 for x in range(10)] generates a list of squares for the numbers 0 to 9.

Benefits of List Comprehension

Here’s why you should consider using list comprehension in Python:

  • Conciseness: List comprehensions reduce the code length. Instead of writing a full loop, you can create the list in a single line.
  • Clarity: For simple operations, list comprehensions can make your code easier to understand at a glance.
  • Performance: List comprehension is faster than traditional loops because it’s optimized for performance under the hood.
